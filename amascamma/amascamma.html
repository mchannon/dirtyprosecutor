Curiouser and curiouser. What’s always made me uneasy about Amazon is how it links accounts between disparate Amazon services, and if you run afoul on one, you can be banned on all. The different services each served a different business, but they’re all named after the same business. Full disclosure: I’ve spent a lot of money on Mechanical Turk (which I haven’t been able to log into for years), apparently I get some link affiliate revenue (probably not very much), of course there’s Amazon Prime, and also I opened an Amazon store a few years back. Maybe it had something to do with the store?

 

First a bit of background. In 2020 I came up with some T-shirt designs and wanted to try my hand at selling T-shirts. If you’ve ever tried something similar, you’ll recognize that Amazon’s pricing is somewhere between exploitative and predatory unless you’re shipping a lot of volume. The challenge is when you’re going from 0 to 1 it’s really hard to know how big 1 is. Like any good follower of Agile, I failed fast with the store. I gave up on the venture shortly thereafter.

 

But then a curious thing happened. A few weeks after Amazon pulled my listings to where they’d no longer show up on search, I got an email with an order. Amazon took their cut, I fulfilled the order. How was that happening?

 

Turns out that Amazon had me in some sort of limbo, where if you searched on Amazon for my stuff, you’d never find it. But if you Googled exactly what I was selling, you could click through and find it on Amazon that way, through Google, even if Amazon itself never returned the result. Yup, just checked it, it still works that way.

 

Google orders happened a few more times over the years, but Amazon always took their cut, and I just kinda shrugged and fulfilled the orders. This last time I fulfilled 4 shirts but forgot to confirm to Amazon that I shipped it, so they clawed back the money (multiple dozens of dollars), and I let them. Picking your battles inevitably means sometimes losing some.

 

Nonetheless, there hadn’t been another Google order in a very long time. In Amazon’s “seller central” web page, after clicking through pages of forms, I unearthed a live chat feature. Finally, I’ll get some answers! (I thought).

 

 

 

 

 

 

Below you’ll see what happens if (as Eram suggested) you try to email them from the place that spawned the chat (gotta love the “BETA” label). An intricate CAPTCHA appeared, and once I solved it, it told me “That is Correct”/”Success!” while it disabled the “Send” button. Maybe it got sent, but I doubt it.

 

 

In my mid-forties I learned a trick that probably comes naturally to millennials and gen-Z’ers. If someone whose job is to help you, doesn’t help you, ask again, this time with someone else. I have to warn you, at this point I’m almost foaming at the mouth and the politeness neurons in my brain flickered out a bit. Not my proudest moment.

Still no clue as to why it was charged or why now, and normally, when you owe somebody something, they let you know about it and give you time to pay it, rather than expropriating it from your account without your knowledge or consent.

 

It also looked like they had found a total of $33.85 and attempted to quadruple bill it to four credit cards, succeeding twice. Even if I did owe them $33.85, I never owed it more than once.

 

And yet, apparently, Mai was only interested in me paying them a fifth time. During the long spells of silence, my imagination pictured her stepping away from her chromebook in a third world internet café, where she had been earning a flat USD0.96 per chat session, smoking a cigarette, and taking a stroll while she considered jumping off a nearby bridge.

You’ll notice I keep referring to Amazon’s Leadership Principles, which you can Google at your leisure, but basically the complete antithesis of the hell I’m enduring here. If Google started out “don’t be evil”, then Amazon started out “the customer is always right”. Maybe it’s some sort of curse that once you become a hegemonic corporation, your brilliant little startup grows to become what it hates most.

 

I might also add that as soon as I clicked “exit chat” it blanked the entirety of our conversation. After having endured similar slings and arrows in the past I learned to cmd-shift-4 often. Imagine if I hadn’t done that. I’d’ve never posted this and you’d have had five minutes of your life back. But aren’t you glad you read my tale of woe? Who doesn't love the dopamine hit from a little bit of schadenfreude?
